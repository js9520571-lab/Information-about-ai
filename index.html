01 — fundamentals

What is Artificial Intelligence?

Artificial Intelligence is the science of making machines that can think, learn, and make decisions — tasks that normally require human intelligence.

At its core, AI systems are trained on massive amounts of data. They find patterns, build internal models of the world, and use those models to make predictions or take actions on new, unseen data.

Modern AI is powered by neural networks — mathematical structures loosely inspired by the human brain, with millions or billions of tunable parameters that adjust during training.

The result: systems that can write code, generate images, translate languages, diagnose diseases, and hold conversations — all from learning patterns in data.

neural_network.py
import torch
import torch.nn as nn

class SimpleAI(nn.Module):
  def __init__(self):
    super().__init__()
    self.brain = nn.Sequential(
      nn.Linear(784, 256),
      nn.ReLU(),
      nn.Linear(256, 10)
    )

  def forward(self, x):
    return self.brain(x)

# Train the model on data
model = SimpleAI()
optimizer = torch.optim.Adam(
  model.parameters(), lr=0.001
)
02 — history

How we got here

70+ years of breakthroughs, failures, and revivals — the story of AI.

1950
Turing Test proposed
Alan Turing asks "Can machines think?" and proposes a test for machine intelligence.
1956
AI coined as a field
John McCarthy coins "Artificial Intelligence" at the Dartmouth Conference.
1997
Deep Blue beats Kasparov
IBM's chess AI defeats the world champion, a watershed moment for symbolic AI.
2012
Deep learning explosion
AlexNet wins ImageNet by a huge margin — deep neural networks take over.
2017
Transformers invented
Google's "Attention Is All You Need" paper invents the architecture behind all modern LLMs.
2022–now
The generative AI era
ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini, Midjourney — AI becomes mainstream and reshapes every industry.

Systems that learn from data without being explicitly programmed. They find patterns and improve with experience.

AlphaGo / OpenAI Five

Autonomous systems that perceive their environment, plan, and take multi-step actions to achieve goals.

06 — how it works

How AI learns

The training pipeline — from raw data to intelligent model.

01
Data collection
Billions of examples — text, images, code — gathered from the internet and curated datasets.
02
Preprocessing
Data is cleaned, tokenized, and formatted. Quality filtering removes noise and harmful content.
03
Training
The model processes data in batches, adjusting billions of parameters to minimize prediction error.
04
Fine-tuning
Human feedback (RLHF) shapes the model to be helpful, harmless, and honest.
05
Deployment
The model is served at scale, handling millions of requests with low latency via APIs.
07 — the future

Where AI is heading

The next decade of AI — breakthroughs, risks, and possibilities.

Near future

Artificial General Intelligence (AGI)

AI that can perform any intellectual task a human can. Most experts believe we are within 5–15 years. The implications for science, medicine, and society are profound.

Emerging now

Multimodal & Embodied AI

AI that sees, hears, speaks, and moves — robots with language understanding, systems that work across all modalities simultaneously.

Challenge

AI Safety & Alignment

Ensuring AI systems remain aligned with human values as they grow more capable. A critical research priority for labs like Anthropic, DeepMind, and OpenAI.

Long term

AI & Scientific Discovery

AI is already solving protein folding (AlphaFold) and discovering new materials. The next frontier: AI-led breakthroughs in physics, medicine, and mathematics.
